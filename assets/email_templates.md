# Email Templates — Outreach Follow-Up Generator
> Ready-to-use templates for Follow-up Steps 1, 2, and 3.
> Replace all [BRACKETED] fields before sending.
> Body word count (excluding greeting/sign-off) must stay under 60 words.

---

## FOLLOW-UP 1 — Soft Value Re-Add

**Strategy**: Prospect is probably busy, not uninterested. Lead with a
timely local hook and re-surface the earlier idea. Keep it breezy and
non-pressuring.

```
Subject: One thing worth 5 minutes, [First Name]

Hi [First Name],

I know your team is navigating the latest [REGULATORY/OPERATIONAL HOOK — 
e.g., "SASO Saber enforcement timelines" / "Dubai Customs smart gate rollout"].
We recently helped [ANONYMIZED REGIONAL DESCRIPTOR] [INDUSTRY DESCRIPTOR] 
[SPECIFIC RESULT — e.g., "automate their WMS matching, saving 12 hours a week"].

I shared some ideas earlier on how we can do the same for [Company Name]. 
Would you be open to a quick look next Tuesday?

Best,
[Sender Name]
```

**A/B Subject Line Options:**
- "[Region] compliance + [outcome] — quick question"
- "Something relevant for [Company Name]'s [pain area]"
- "Thought of you when I saw [hook reference]"

---

## FOLLOW-UP 2 — New Angle

**Strategy**: Assume the first message didn't land or wasn't opened. Come
in with a completely different angle — new pain point, fresh case study,
or different framing of the value. Acknowledge you've reached out before
but don't dwell on it.

```
Subject: Different angle, [First Name] — worth 2 minutes

Hi [First Name],

[NEW REGULATORY/OPERATIONAL HOOK — different from Follow-up 1].
We helped [ANONYMIZED REGIONAL DESCRIPTOR] [INDUSTRY DESCRIPTOR] 
[DIFFERENT SPECIFIC RESULT — e.g., "reduce customs clearance errors by 40%
in the first quarter post-integration"].

We spoke briefly [TIME REFERENCE — e.g., "last week" / "a few weeks back"]. 
Would [SPECIFIC DAY, e.g., "Thursday morning"] work to walk through how 
this applies to [Company Name]?

Best,
[Sender Name]
```

**A/B Subject Line Options:**
- "Post-[hook event] — a quick idea for [role/company]"
- "[Metric] result we got for a [region] [industry] firm"
- "Another way to think about [pain point]"

---

## FOLLOW-UP 3 — Break-Up Note

**Strategy**: This is the last message in the sequence for now. Keep it
short, warm, and with zero pressure. Leave the door open without chasing.
The goal is to exit gracefully and stay top-of-mind for when the timing
is right.

```
Subject: Last note for now, [First Name]

Hi [First Name],

I'll keep this brief — I know your plate is full, especially with 
[BRIEF CONTEXT HOOK — e.g., "the ongoing customs digitization push in [Region]"].

If [Company Name]'s [PAIN AREA] isn't a priority right now, no worries 
at all. I'll leave the door open — feel free to reach out whenever the 
timing works.

Best,
[Sender Name]
```

**A/B Subject Line Options:**
- "Leaving this here for when the timing is right"
- "No pressure — just leaving the door open"
- "Signing off for now, [First Name]"

## WORD COUNT TOOL (Manual)

Count words in the body only (between "Hi [First Name]," and "Best,").
If over 60 words:
1. Remove adjectives first ("significant", "real", "meaningful")
2. Collapse two-sentence proof points into one
3. Tighten the hook — you rarely need more than one sentence
4. Shorten the CTA — "Quick look next Tuesday?" is enough
